DGA Service Scorecard

Answer a few questions to find the right service level for your project.

Step 1 of 2 — Service Fit

Commercial Complexity

How much does design development impact cost?

Consider asking
  • How developed is the design at this stage (concept, planning, or technical)?
  • Are there any elements still being explored that could significantly change cost?
  • Are there specialist items or bespoke features that are not yet defined?
  • Has the client expressed interest in upgrading materials or specification during design?
  • Are there planning or heritage constraints that may drive design changes later?
1 Minimal — straightforward scope
2 Some — moderate design evolution expected
3 Significant — complex/evolving design with major cost implications

Budget Sensitivity

How important is ongoing cost clarity?

Consider asking
  • How sensitive is the project to budget changes?
  • Has the client set a strict cost limit that cannot move?
  • Has the client previously expressed concern about affordability?
  • Does the architect want frequent cost reassurance as the design develops?
  • Is the project dependent on financial approvals or funding thresholds?
1 Low — client comfortable with estimates
2 Moderate — periodic updates needed
3 High — tight budget, real-time cost control required

Delivery Coordination Complexity

How much active coordination is needed?

Consider asking
  • How many consultants or specialists are involved in the design team?
  • Are there multiple stakeholders influencing decisions (client representatives, family members, advisers)?
  • Is the programme tight or driven by external deadlines?
  • Are there known areas where design coordination may be challenging?
  • Will the project require frequent design workshops or coordination meetings?
1 Simple — single contractor, standard programme
2 Moderate — few trades, some coordination
3 Complex — multiple packages, phased delivery

Client Experience & Decision Structure

Client confidence in managing delivery?

Consider asking
  • Has the client delivered projects like this before?
  • Do they have a clear decision-making structure?
  • Are they comfortable making timely decisions when needed?
  • Do they have internal support (project manager, adviser, family office)?
  • Do they appear uncertain about the construction or procurement process?
1 Experienced — client can manage independently
2 Some experience — needs periodic guidance
3 Limited — needs hands-on support throughout

Architectural Reliance on Commercial Support

Architect's reliance on cost/contract support?

Consider asking
  • Has the architect asked us to help explain costs to the client?
  • Are there areas where the design team anticipates difficult cost discussions?
  • Does the architect expect us to help manage expectations around budget?
  • Is the client already questioning cost or value?
  • Would the architect prefer us to take the lead in presenting cost information?
1 Low — architect handles commercial matters
2 Moderate — needs support at key stages
3 High — needs continuous commercial partnership
